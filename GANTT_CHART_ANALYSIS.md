## RECOMMENDED BEST PRACTICES FOR YOUR POWERPOINT GANTT CHART

### Layout & Organization
1. **Use 7-Phase Structure:**
   - Program Setup & Admin (Semester 1)
   - Year 1 Coursework (Year 1)
   - Year 2 Coursework (Year 2)
   - Preliminary Examination (End of Year 2)
   - Dissertation Proposal (Year 2 → 3)
   - Research & Writing (Years 3-4)
   - Final Defense & Graduation (Year 4+)

2. **Timeline Granularity:**
   - Semester-level is sufficient (avoid week-by-week in PowerPoint)
   - Use years for phase headers
   - Show specific due dates for key milestones

### Visual Design (PowerPoint-Specific)
1. **Color Coding by Phase:**
   - Phase 1: Light blue (admin/setup)
   - Phase 2-3: Light green (coursework)
   - Phase 4: Light yellow (exams)
   - Phase 5: Light orange (proposal)
   - Phase 6: Light purple (research)
   - Phase 7: Light gray (final steps)

2. **Timeline Bar Using Shapes:**
   - Use PowerPoint rectangles/bars to show duration
   - Thicker bars for major phases
   - Use arrows for dependencies

3. **Task Grouping:**
   - Group related courses (e.g., all coursework together)
   - Use indentation or nested bullets for sub-tasks
   - Separate admin tasks from academic tasks with visual separation

### Content Elements to Include
1. **Course Information:**
   - Course number AND title (e.g., "TECH 676 - Analysis of Research Methods")
   - Credit hours (as rubric requires)
   - Semester/year offered

2. **Administrative Milestones:**
   - EPOS submission, CITI training, RCR completion
   - Committee formation/updates
   - IRB approval
   - GS Forms (8 for prelim, 8 for final exam, 9 for thesis)

3. **Dissertation Phases:**
   - Prelim exam components labeled separately (Written/Oral)
   - Proposal defense as distinct milestone
   - Data collection dates (if applicable)
   - Defense date
   - Post-defense requirements

4. **Key Dependencies:**
   - Show that courses must complete before prelims
   - Show that prelims must complete before proposal
   - Show IRB approval required before data collection

### Rubric Alignment Check
- [ ] All coursework clearly listed with credit hours
- [ ] Prelim exam placement shown with date range
- [ ] Proposal placement shown with timeline
- [ ] Research/data collection semesters explicitly labeled
- [ ] Final defense date specified with buffer for revisions
- [ ] All 7 phases visually distinct and labeled
- [ ] Timeline shows realistic duration (3-5+ years)
- [ ] Dependencies visible

### PowerPoint Layout Tips
1. **Use Multiple Slides if Needed:**
   - Slide 1: Phases 1-3 (Setup & Coursework)
   - Slide 2: Phases 4-5 (Exams & Proposal)
   - Slide 3: Phases 6-7 (Research & Defense)
   - Or combine all on one landscape slide

2. **Use Table Format:**
   - 4-5 columns: Phase | Semester | Key Tasks | Duration | Dependencies
   - Use cell colors for visual separation

3. **Use SmartArt (if available):**
   - Process diagram showing flow through phases
   - With timeline at bottom showing years

4. **Alternative: Process Flow + Timeline:**
   - Top section: Process flow boxes (7 phases)
   - Bottom section: Timeline strip showing Years 1-5
   - Middle section: Connect boxes to timeline positions

## FINAL RECOMMENDATIONS

**For Your PowerPoint Gantt Chart:**

1. **Adopt Tara's 7-Phase Structure** - it's comprehensive and clear
2. **Use Semester/Year Timeline** - simpler than week-by-week for PowerPoint
3. **Incorporate Administrative Items** - shows understanding of Purdue requirements
4. **Create Clear Dependencies** - arrows showing prerequisite relationships
5. **Color-Code Phases** - visually distinct section identification
6. **Include All Courses** - with numbers and credit hours
7. **Highlight Milestone Dates** - prelim, proposal, defense with specific timeframes
8. **Show Rubric Compliance** - ensure all 5 rubric elements are clearly visible

**Color Scheme Suggestion for PowerPoint:**
- Admin (Phase 1): Purdue Gold/Blue
- Coursework (Phases 2-3): Green shades (increasing intensity)
- Exams (Phase 4): Yellow/Amber
- Proposal (Phase 5): Orange
- Research (Phase 6): Purple
- Final (Phase 7): Gray

**Avoid:**
- Overly complex week-by-week detail (PowerPoint not ideal for this)
- Vague date ranges without specific milestones
- Missing credit hour information
- Unclear phase boundaries
- Dependencies that aren't labeled

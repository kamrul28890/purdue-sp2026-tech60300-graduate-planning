Preliminary Handbook Task ListPrerequisitesSecure an approved plan of study.Satisfactorily complete formal study, maintaining good academic standing with at least a 3.0 GPA and no unresolved grades below a B- in core courses.Satisfy any departmental world language requirements.Finalize a preliminary examining committee of at least three graduate faculty members , ensuring a majority hold regular graduate faculty certification.Administrative TasksSubmit the reading list to the preliminary exam committee for approval exactly 9 weeks before the exam.Confirm the scheduled dates for the written and oral components with the committee.Submit written exam responses immediately upon completion , which must be done within six weeks of reading list approval.If both components are approved, ensure the Chair submits electronic Form 10 to the Graduate School.Preparation TasksMeet with your advisor to discuss your research topic and preliminary exam timeline.Develop a reading list related to your research topic at least 10 weeks before the exam.Create a study plan focused on synthesizing and integrating knowledge , reviewing literature with an eye toward critiquing theoretical integration, design, and applicability.Prepare for the oral component by anticipating follow-up questions from the committee that extend or clarify your written responses.Transition TasksSchedule your dissertation proposal defense to finalize your research design and methods.Transition into full research semesters dedicated to intensive data collection and analysis.
